September 19, 2021: I am absolutely thrilled to be raising awareness and funds for Juneteenth, Santa Cruz, and for Music in May, and to be uniting them as well- in spirit, not officially- next Spring as I collaborate with my Juneteenth Collective to mentor and educate underserved youth in our Harmony Choir for a concert event that will be an outreach of Music in May.

I am also just beyond honored that both of the concerts are Black and BIPOC positive events: on November 21, half of performers represent BIPOC. In these times, it is a joy to note such organic relationships and to both intentionally create and to normalize such expansiveness. On April 10th, not only does Diva Leberta Lorál again bring more inclusivity to our stage ,but ,once again, Black, Female, and Black Female composers will be richly represented.

Personally, I am so blessed to be continuing on my lifelong musical journey with the works of the featured composers and with my Musical Soulmates, with personal excellence at the fore while proudly AND humbly serving Music in May and Juneteenth, Santa Cruz.

Many of you are aware of my immersion on Gospel Music. On October 9th, I will step aside as pianist and welcome, as producer, to the Musical Soulmates stage, top of the line, world class Gospel, including our own Tammi Brown,and Oakland’s very own Terrance Kelly and his Oakland Interfaith Gospel Ensemble. I have been influenced and inspired by each of these artist, who are friends who and are sharing the stage (at the Resource Center for Nonviolence in Santa Cruz and Digitally as well) to support and raise funds for Michelle Nash. Michelle Nash is my Musical Sister, Inspiration and Friend, who can be seen singing herself on my website.
